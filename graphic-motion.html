AFP Digital Media (2017-19)

Below is most of my work for the media course I took during from Years 9 to 11. I have decided to order them chronologically to emphasise my growth over the three years. I will say, though, that the one thing in common between my work in Year 9 and that in Year 11 is that the endings are consistently crap, because when I leave everything to the last minute, I do that in a way that makes the start of the video amazing and leaves the end of the video in the dirt.


Of all the projects I did, the video essay and the client commission were by far my favourite ones (last two videos) :)

(2017) First ever project, a hashtag advertising campaign, highlighting the inevitability of imperfection. Aptly far from perfect, and probably the last video I edited with ScreenFlow.

(2018) Animated intro for our TV production Slime Time. Graphics in Photoshop and video in After Effects. First time properly using AE...

(2018) Video essay on the reporting of the Iran Nuclear Deal.

(2017) Mini-documentary on Parkour and Freerunning in Hong Kong. At the time I watched a lot of parkour and freerunning videos, and my passion for this topic led me to join the Hong Kong Parkour Association for a training session at Kowloon Park.

(2018) Role: Vision Mixer (operating the TriCaster). Made the intro, outro, and graphics.

(2018) Award-winning Client Commission project. Part of the reason it had scored so highly was because it had turned from a four-person project into a two-person project the week before the deadline. Used Blender for the first time for a big project like this. Special thanks to my good friend Carlynda who agreed to "star" in some of the shots and was responsible for sound.
